History

1971

First Starbucks Opens

Starbucks was founded in Seattle, Washington by Jerry Baldwin, Zev Siegl, and Gordon Bowker. The original store sold coffee beans and equipment rather than prepared drinks.

1982

Howard Schultz Joins Starbucks

Howard Schultz joined Starbucks as the Director of Retail Operations and Marketing and became interested in bringing Italian coffee culture to America.

1987

New Ownership

Howard Schultz purchased Starbucks and began expanding the company beyond Seattle.

1992

Going Public

Starbucks became a publicly traded company on NASDAQ, allowing the company to grow rapidly.

1996

International Expansion

The first Starbucks store outside North America opened in Tokyo, Japan.

2003

Seattle's Best Coffee

Starbucks acquired Seattle's Best Coffee and expanded its presence even further.

2008

Return of Howard Schultz

Howard Schultz returned as CEO and introduced changes to improve customer experience and coffee quality.

2011

40th Anniversary

Starbucks celebrated its 40th anniversary and introduced a refreshed logo design.

Today

A Global Brand

Starbucks has grown into one of the world's largest coffeehouse chains, with tens of thousands of stores serving customers around the globe.
